# ScanFirst — Disclaimer

_Last updated: 12 July 2026_

**ScanFirst is a safety aid, not a guarantee of safety.**

ScanFirst checks every scan against threat databases, link destinations, and
known scam patterns, and explains what it finds in plain language. This helps
you spot many risky QR codes and links before they open — but:

- **No automated tool can detect every threat.** New scams, freshly created
  phishing pages, and novel attack techniques may not be recognized.
- **Threat data changes constantly.** The databases ScanFirst relies on
  (OpenPhish, URLhaus, and others) are updated over time; a link that is clean
  today may be malicious tomorrow, and vice versa.
- **A "Looks safe" result means our checks found no problems.** It is **not** a
  promise that the code, link, website, file, or payment is safe.

**You remain responsible for your own decisions.** In particular:

- Verify the **payee, amount, and destination** yourself before continuing any
  payment.
- Remember the golden rule: **receiving money never requires scanning a QR code
  or entering your PIN.** If a code asks you to pay when you expected to receive,
  stop.
- Treat unexpected links, downloads, and login prompts with caution even when
  ScanFirst reports no problems.
